## Engagement Behavior Shifts

The way people engage is changing rapidly. Key shifts:

### Shares > Likes
- TikTok: Shares per post up **+45%**
- Instagram: Shares per post up **+12%**
- DM sharing is now Instagram's strongest algorithmic signal
- Twitter: Retweets have **20x** algorithmic weight vs likes

**Implication**: Optimize for content people want to SEND to someone. The "DM test" -- would someone screenshot this and send it to a friend?

### Comments Declining
- TikTok: Comments per post down **-24%**
- Instagram: Comments per post down **-16%**
- Shift toward passive engagement (saves, shares) over active engagement (comments)

**Implication**: Don't measure success by comments alone. Saves and shares are increasingly the real signals.

### Saves / Bookmarks Rising
- Saves are rising across all platforms as "save for later" behavior grows
- Instagram saves are a top algorithmic signal
- Twitter bookmarks weight 10x in algorithm

**Implication**: Create "save-worthy" content -- frameworks, checklists, reference materials, step-by-steps.

## Engagement Rate Calculation

### Standard Formula
```
Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Followers x 100
```

### By Reach (More Accurate)
```
Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Reach x 100
```

## Content Performance Tiers

Categorize content to understand what works:

### Tier 1: Viral (top 5%)
- 5x+ your average engagement
- Significant follower growth
- **Action**: Reverse engineer the pattern. What hook? What topic? What format? Create variations.

### Tier 2: Strong (top 20%)
- 2-3x your average engagement
- Above-average saves/shares
- **Action**: This is your sweet spot. Double down on these topics and formats.

### Tier 3: Average (middle 50%)
- Close to your average engagement
- Expected performance
- **Action**: Keep what works, experiment with hooks and formatting.

### Tier 4: Underperforming (bottom 25%)
- Below 50% of your average
- Low engagement across all metrics
- **Action**: Analyze why. Bad hook? Wrong timing? Wrong topic? Wrong format? Learn and move on.

## Analytics Review Cadence

### Daily (5 min)
- Check notifications and respond to comments
- Note any unusually performing content

### Weekly (30 min)
- Review past week's content performance
- Identify top performer and worst performer
- Plan next week based on patterns
- Track follower growth

### Monthly (1 hour)
- Analyze 30-day engagement trends by format, topic, and posting time
- Calculate month-over-month growth rates
- Update content calendar based on data
- Review conversion metrics (social > website > signups)

### Quarterly (2 hours)
- Full content audit -- which pillars are working?
- Platform ROI assessment -- where is time best spent?
- Audience growth analysis
- Update strategy based on what the data says
- Set goals for next quarter
