## Content Pillars

### How to Identify Your 3-5 Pillars

1. **Product-led**: What problems does your product solve?
2. **Audience-led**: What does your ICP need to learn?
3. **Search-led**: What topics have volume in your space?
4. **Competitor-led**: What are competitors ranking for?

### Pillar Criteria

Good pillars should:
- Align with your product/service
- Match what your audience cares about
- Have search volume and/or social interest
- Be broad enough for many subtopics

**Algorithms reward topic consistency.** LinkedIn explicitly tracks it, YouTube evaluates channels holistically, TikTok builds micro-community clusters. Picking 3-5 pillars and going deep beats being broad.

### Pillar Structure

```
Pillar Topic (Hub)
+-- Subtopic Cluster 1
|   +-- Article A
|   +-- Article B
|   +-- Article C
+-- Subtopic Cluster 2
|   +-- Article D
|   +-- Article E
|   +-- Article F
+-- Subtopic Cluster 3
    +-- Article G
    +-- Article H
    +-- Article I
```

Most content works fine under `/blog` with good internal linking. Only use dedicated hub/spoke URL structures (`/guides/topic`) for major topics with layered depth.

## Searchable vs Shareable

Every piece of content should be searchable, shareable, or both. Search content compounds; shareable content creates demand.

| Type | Purpose | Strategy |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Searchable** | Capture existing demand | Target keywords, match search intent, structured for ranking |
| **Shareable** | Create demand | Novel insights, emotional triggers, stories, data |
| **Both** | Maximum value | Searchable foundation + shareable angle |

### Searchable Content Checklist
- Target a specific keyword or question
- Match search intent exactly
- Use clear titles that match queries
- Structure with headings that mirror search patterns
- Comprehensive coverage (don't leave questions unanswered)
- Include data, examples, and authoritative sources

### Shareable Content Checklist
- Lead with novel insight, original data, or counterintuitive take
- Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence
- Tell stories that make people feel something
- Create content people share to look smart or help others
- Pass the "DM test" -- would someone send this to a specific friend?
- Aim for 2+ STEPPS elements (Social Currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical Value, Stories)

## Content Types Reference

### Searchable Content Types

**Use-Case Content**
Formula: `[persona] + [use-case]`. Targets long-tail keywords.
- "Project management for designers"
- "Task tracking for developers"

**Hub and Spoke**
Hub = comprehensive overview. Spokes = related subtopics. Create hub first, then build spokes with interlinking.

**Template Libraries**
High-intent keywords + product adoption. Target "X template" searches. Provide standalone value + show how product enhances it.

### Shareable Content Types

**Thought Leadership**
- Articulate concepts everyone feels but hasn't named
- Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence
- Share vulnerable, honest experiences

**Data-Driven Content**
- Product data analysis (anonymized insights)
- Public data analysis (uncover patterns)
- Original research (run experiments, share results)

**Expert Roundups**
15-30 experts answering one specific question. Built-in distribution -- each expert shares it.

**Case Studies**
Structure: Challenge > Solution > Results > Key learnings

**Meta Content / Build-in-Public**
Behind-the-scenes transparency: "How We Got Our First $5k MRR," "Why We Chose Debt Over VC."

## Keyword Research by Buyer Stage

Map content to the buyer's journey:

### Awareness Stage
**Modifiers**: "what is," "how to," "guide to," "introduction to"
- "What is Agile Project Management"
- "Guide to Sprint Planning"

### Consideration Stage
**Modifiers**: "best," "top," "vs," "alternatives," "comparison"
- "Best Project Management Tools for Remote Teams"
- "Asana vs Trello vs Monday"

### Decision Stage
**Modifiers**: "pricing," "reviews," "demo," "trial," "buy"
- "Project Management Tool Pricing Comparison"
- "[Product] Reviews"

### Implementation Stage
**Modifiers**: "templates," "examples," "tutorial," "how to use," "setup"
- "Project Template Library"
- "Step-by-Step Setup Tutorial"

## Content Ideation Sources

### 1. Keyword Data
Analyze exports from Ahrefs, SEMrush, GSC for:
- Topic clusters (group related keywords)
- Buyer stage mapping
- Quick wins (low competition + decent volume + high relevance)
- Content gaps (keywords competitors rank for)

### 2. Customer Conversations
Extract from sales calls, support tickets, interviews:
- Questions asked > FAQ content or blog posts
- Pain points > problems in their own words
- Objections > content to address proactively
- Language patterns > exact phrases to use (voice of customer)

### 3. Forum Research
- **Reddit**: `site:reddit.com [topic]` -- top posts, frustrations, upvoted answers
- **Quora**: `site:quora.com [topic]` -- most-followed questions
- **Other**: Indie Hackers, HN, Product Hunt, industry Slack/Discord
- Extract: FAQs, misconceptions, debates, terminology

### 4. Competitor Analysis
- `site:competitor.com/blog` -- find their content
- Identify top-performing posts, topics covered repeatedly, gaps
- Look for topics you can cover better or angles they miss

### 5. Viral Content Analysis
- What's performing in your niche on social?
- Which hooks are working? Which formats?
- See `references/reverse-engineering.md` for the full process

## Prioritizing Content Ideas

Score each idea on four weighted factors:

### 1. Customer Impact (40%)
- How frequently did this topic come up in research?
- What percentage of customers face this challenge?
- How emotionally charged was this pain point?

### 2. Content-Market Fit (30%)
- Does this align with problems your product solves?
- Can you offer unique insights?
- Will this naturally lead to product interest?

### 3. Search Potential (20%)
- Monthly search volume?
- Competition level?
- Related long-tail opportunities?

### 4. Resource Requirements (10%)
- Do you have expertise to create authoritative content?
- What additional research/assets needed?

### Scoring Template

| Idea | Impact (40%) | Fit (30%) | Search (20%) | Resources (10%) | Total |
|------|-------------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| Topic A | 8 | 9 | 7 | 6 | 8.0 |
| Topic B | 6 | 7 | 9 | 8 | 7.1 |

## The Waterfall Repurposing System

The core principle: **Write once, repurpose 10 times.** Turn 4 hours of work per week into lasting content across multiple channels.

### The Flow

```
Long-Form Source (blog, video, podcast, newsletter)
  |
  +-> Twitter/X thread (extract 5-10 key points)
  +-> LinkedIn post (professional framing, business impact)
  +-> Instagram carousel (visual, educational, save-worthy)
  +-> TikTok/Reels clips (talking head or visual, 30-60s)
  +-> YouTube Shorts (vertical clips from long-form video)
  +-> Email newsletter excerpt
  +-> Quote graphics (2-3 standout insights as images)
  +-> Audiograms (if podcast source)
  +-> Reddit post (educational framing, community-first)
```

### Platform-Specific Adaptation

Same insight, different execution. **Never just copy-paste.**

| Source | Derivative | Adaptation |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Blog post | Twitter thread | Extract key points, add punchy commentary |
| Blog post | LinkedIn post | Professional framing, add personal experience |
| Blog post | Instagram carousel | Visual, one point per slide, design-forward |
| YouTube video | 5-10 TikTok/Reels clips | Vertical, subtitles, hooks per clip |
| Podcast episode | Audiograms + quote graphics + show notes blog | Multiple formats from one session |
| Webinar | Blog post + clips + email series + quotes | Full content ecosystem |
| Newsletter | Twitter thread + LinkedIn post | Platform-native tone |

### AI-Powered Repurposing

AI reduces manual repurposing from ~9 hours to 1-2 hours (90%+ time savings):
- **Text adaptation**: Reframe tone for each platform
- **Caption variants**: Generate 3-5 options per post
- **Key quote extraction**: Pull most shareable lines from long-form
- **Summary generation**: Create TL;DRs and abstracts

**Human must own**: Voice authenticity, fact-checking, platform-specific nuance, personal stories.

## Content Batching Workflow

### The System

1. **Monthly** (1-2 hours): Plan themes and pillar topics aligned to business goals
2. **Weekly** (2-3 hours): Batch-create 5-7 posts in one session
3. **Daily** (15-20 minutes): Engagement -- comments, replies, community participation
4. **Quarterly** (2 hours): Review analytics, refine strategy, adjust content pillars

### Weekly Batching Session

A focused 2-3 hour session can produce an entire week of content:

**Hour 1: Create source content**
- Write one long-form piece (blog, newsletter, or script)
- Or record one video/podcast episode

**Hour 2: Repurpose**
- Extract 5-10 key points for social posts
- Write hooks for each platform
- Create carousel outlines or video clip lists

**Hour 3: Polish and schedule**
- Edit and refine each post
- Schedule using native scheduling or tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, etc.)
- Prepare engagement prompts and CTA variants

### Solopreneur Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Content | Time |
|-----|---------|------|
| **Monday** | Awareness/educational post | 15 min engagement |
| **Tuesday** | Tactical how-to or framework | 15 min engagement |
| **Wednesday** | Personal story or behind-the-scenes | 15 min engagement |
| **Thursday** | Community engagement (questions, polls) | 20 min engagement |
| **Friday** | Newsletter tease / CTA | 15 min engagement |
| **Saturday** | Repurpose best-performing content | Rest |
| **Sunday** | **Batch session**: plan + create next week | 2-3 hours |

## Editorial Calendar

### Monthly Planning

| Week | Focus | Content Mix |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Searchable content | 2 blog posts targeting keywords |
| 2 | Shareable content | 1 thought leadership + 1 data piece |
| 3 | Searchable content | 2 blog posts (use-case or template) |
| 4 | Mixed | 1 case study + 1 roundup |

### Content Production Workflow

1. **Ideation** (Week -2): Score and select topics
2. **Brief** (Week -1): Outline, keyword targets, sources
3. **Draft** (Week 0): Write first draft
4. **Edit** (Week 0+1): Review, refine, optimize
5. **Publish** (Week 0+2): Publish + distribute
6. **Promote** (Week 0+2-3): Social distribution, email, outreach
7. **Measure** (Week +4): Track performance, iterate

### Calendar Entry Fields

Every post entry should include:
- Date, time, and timezone
- Platform(s)
- Content type (text, image, video, carousel, story)
- Content pillar it maps to
- Copy and visuals
- Links, hashtags, and tags
- Status (draft, scheduled, published)
- Performance tracking fields

### Launch Campaign Calendar

For product launches (Product Hunt, HN, etc.), overlay a special calendar:

| Timeline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **-30 days** | Start engaging on launch platforms (PH, Reddit, HN comments) |
| **-14 days** | Teaser content on social (build-in-public updates) |
| **-7 days** | Pre-launch landing page, email list activation |
| **-3 days** | Final asset preparation, supporter outreach |
| **Launch day** | Coordinate launch + social amplification |
| **+1-3 days** | Thank supporters, share results, engage comments |
| **+7 days** | Retrospective post (what worked, what didn't) |
| **+14 days** | Follow-up content based on launch feedback |
