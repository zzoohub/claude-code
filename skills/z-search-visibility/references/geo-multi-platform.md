# GEO: Multi-Platform Presence Reference

How to build brand visibility across the platforms AI systems draw from, beyond your website.

---

## Why Multi-Platform Matters for AI Visibility

AI systems assemble answers from multiple sources. When generating a response, they do not only check your website. They pull information from YouTube, Reddit, review sites, LinkedIn, industry publications, news outlets, podcasts (transcripts), and more.

Reddit, LinkedIn, and YouTube are consistently among the top cited sources by major LLMs. A brand that only exists on its own website has a fraction of the surface area compared to one present across multiple trusted platforms.

Two types of presence drive AI visibility:
1. **Owned presence**: Content you create on external platforms
2. **Earned mentions**: References to your brand from independent sources

Both are necessary. Owned presence demonstrates expertise. Earned mentions validate credibility. Together, they give AI systems the confidence to reference you.

---

## Owned Presence: Platform Strategies

### YouTube

**Why it matters for AI**: YouTube content is frequently cited in AI answers, especially for how-to queries, product comparisons, and educational topics. Google AI Overviews can also pull from YouTube content since Google owns the platform.

**What to create**:
- Product demos and tutorials
- Industry explainers and educational content
- Comparison videos (your product vs. alternatives)
- Expert interviews and thought leadership
- Customer success stories / case studies

**Optimization for AI extraction**:
- Write detailed, keyword-rich video descriptions (AI systems read these)
- Use descriptive titles that match search queries
- Add chapters with timestamps (helps AI identify relevant segments)
- Include transcripts or enable auto-captions (AI reads transcripts)
- Pin a comment with key takeaways and links

### LinkedIn

**Why it matters for AI**: LinkedIn is a top-cited source in LLM outputs. It signals professional authority and expertise.

**What to create**:
- Company page with complete, accurate description aligned with website
- Executive thought leadership posts and articles
- Industry analysis and commentary
- Behind-the-scenes content showing expertise in action
- LinkedIn newsletters on core topics

**Optimization for AI extraction**:
- Use clear, descriptive headlines on articles
- Include specific data and insights (not just opinions)
- Write self-contained posts, each should make sense on its own
- Complete all company page fields: description, specialties, industry

### Reddit

**Why it matters for AI**: Reddit is heavily cited by AI systems, especially for authentic opinions, product recommendations, and community-vetted advice. ChatGPT and Perplexity frequently reference Reddit threads.

**What to do**:
- Participate authentically in relevant subreddits (do not overtly promote)
- Answer questions where your expertise genuinely helps
- Share knowledge and insights, with your brand as context not pitch
- Engage in AMAs (Ask Me Anything) if relevant to your industry
- Monitor brand mentions and participate in conversations about your product

**What NOT to do**:
- Do not spam promotional content
- Do not use fake accounts or astroturf
- Do not just post links to your site without adding value to the discussion

### Industry Publications and Guest Content

**Why it matters for AI**: Articles on established publications carry editorial credibility that AI systems weight heavily.

**What to pursue**:
- Guest articles on industry blogs and publications
- Contributed expert commentary in news articles (Connectively, Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer)
- Original research reports that journalists and other publishers cite
- Podcast guest appearances (transcripts become searchable content)
- Conference presentations (slides and recordings become citable content)
- Webinars with industry partners

### Other Owned Platforms

- **Quora**: Answer questions in your area of expertise. Quora answers appear in AI responses.
- **Medium / Substack**: Publish thought leadership and in-depth analysis
- **GitHub** (for tech companies): Open-source contributions, documentation, technical content
- **Product Hunt** (for product launches): Product descriptions and discussions

## Earned Mentions: Building Third-Party References

### Review Platforms

Reviews are a primary source AI systems use when recommending products.

**Key platforms by industry**:
- **B2B SaaS**: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, GetApp
- **E-commerce**: Amazon reviews, Trustpilot, product-specific review sites
- **Local business**: Google Business Profile reviews, Yelp
- **Apps**: App Store reviews, Google Play reviews

**Strategy**:
- Actively solicit reviews from satisfied customers
- Respond to reviews (shows engagement, adds context AI can read)
- Address negative reviews constructively (AI reads sentiment)
- Ensure your product category and description are correct on review platforms

### Press and Media Coverage

**How to earn it**:
- Digital PR: Create newsworthy content (original research, industry firsts, trend analysis)
- Respond to journalist queries via platforms like Connectively (formerly HARO), Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer
- Build relationships with industry journalists and analysts
- Issue press releases for significant milestones (with genuine news value)
- Offer expert commentary on industry trends and breaking news

### Community Mentions

Organic recommendations from real users carry strong credibility signals.

**How to encourage**:
- Build a product worth talking about (no shortcut for this)
- Create a community around your product (forums, Slack/Discord, user groups)
- Highlight customer success stories that users want to share
- Make it easy for customers to recommend you (referral programs, shareable content)
- Engage with users who mention you organically

## Sentiment Matters

AI systems do not just count mentions. They assess sentiment. A brand frequently mentioned in negative context can appear in AI answers with negative framing.

### Monitoring Sentiment

Track how your brand is discussed across:
- Review platform ratings and review text
- Reddit and forum discussions
- Social media mentions
- Press coverage tone
- AI answer sentiment (how AI platforms frame your brand when mentioning it)

### Managing Sentiment

- Address recurring complaints in your product/service (the best sentiment strategy is having a good product)
- Respond to negative reviews and criticism constructively and publicly
- Create content that directly addresses common concerns or misconceptions
- Amplify positive experiences (case studies, testimonials, user-generated content)
- Do not try to suppress negative content. Address the underlying issues.

## Multi-Platform Content Strategy

### Core Content, Multiple Formats

Create core content once, then adapt for multiple platforms:

1. **Original research report** (your website)
2. Key findings as a **LinkedIn article** and posts
3. Walkthrough as a **YouTube video**
4. Discussion thread on **Reddit** (share insights, invite discussion)
5. Pitch to **journalists** for coverage
6. Excerpt on **Medium/Substack** with link to full report
7. Slides on **Speaker Deck** for reference

Each platform version should be native to that platform (do not just copy-paste), but the core message and data stay consistent.

### Platform Prioritization

You do not need to be everywhere. Prioritize based on:

1. **Where your audience is**: If your buyers are not on Reddit, do not invest heavily there
2. **Where AI systems pull from**: YouTube, Reddit, LinkedIn, and major review platforms are consistently cited
3. **Where you can be authentic**: Do not force a presence on platforms where you cannot add genuine value
4. **Resource availability**: Better to be excellent on 3 platforms than mediocre on 10

### Consistency Requirements

Across all platforms, maintain:
- Consistent brand positioning and messaging (see Entity Clarity reference)
- Consistent quality bar
- Consistent publishing cadence
- Consistent engagement, do not just broadcast, participate in conversations

## Multi-Platform Audit Checklist

### Owned Presence
- [ ] YouTube channel with relevant, optimized content
- [ ] LinkedIn company page complete and aligned with website
- [ ] Executive/team thought leadership active on LinkedIn
- [ ] Presence in relevant subreddits (authentic participation, not promotion)
- [ ] Guest content on industry publications
- [ ] Podcast appearances or own podcast
- [ ] Conference presentations/webinars

### Earned Mentions
- [ ] Reviews on relevant platforms (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, etc.)
- [ ] Average review rating and volume competitive with alternatives
- [ ] Press/media coverage for brand or key people
- [ ] Organic community mentions in forums, Reddit, Quora
- [ ] Backlinks from authoritative industry sources
- [ ] Brand mentions in industry reports, roundups, comparisons

### Sentiment
- [ ] Overall sentiment is positive or neutral across platforms
- [ ] Negative feedback is addressed constructively
- [ ] No significant recurring complaints going unaddressed
- [ ] AI answer sentiment tracked (how AI platforms frame your brand)

### Gaps
- [ ] Identified platforms where competitors have presence but you do not
- [ ] Identified content formats competitors use that you do not
- [ ] Prioritized 2-3 highest-impact gaps to address first
