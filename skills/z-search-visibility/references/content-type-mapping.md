# Content Type Mapping Reference

Which optimization strategies to apply to which content. Not every page needs every optimization. Applying the wrong optimization to the wrong content wastes effort and can hurt the content's primary purpose.

---

## Core Principle: Purpose First, Optimization Second

Every piece of content has a primary purpose. Optimization should support that purpose, never override it. A brand manifesto that reads like an FAQ has lost its soul. A technical guide that prioritizes poetic language over clarity has failed its users.

Before optimizing any content, ask:
1. **What is the primary purpose of this content?** (inform, persuade, convert, entertain, guide, build trust, etc.)
2. **Who is the audience?** (new visitors, existing customers, investors, media, internal team, etc.)
3. **How will people find this content?** (search, direct link, internal navigation, social share, AI recommendation, etc.)
4. **What would "success" look like for this page?** (traffic, conversions, time on page, brand recall, snippet ownership, AI citation, etc.)

The answers determine which optimization layers apply and how aggressively.

---

## Optimization Intensity Scale

Each content type is mapped using a four-level scale for each optimization layer:

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Full** | Apply all relevant techniques from this optimization layer. This is a primary optimization target. |
| **Moderate** | Apply core fundamentals but do not over-optimize. Balance with content purpose. |
| **Light** | Apply only basics (meta tags, basic structure). Do not let optimization affect tone or creative direction. |
| **None** | Do not optimize for this layer. The content purpose would be harmed by optimization. |

## Content Purpose Mapping

Beyond page type, content purpose affects optimization approach. The same page type can serve different purposes.

### Purpose: Inform and Educate

**Optimization approach**: Full SEO + Full AEO + Full GEO

This is the sweet spot for all three optimization layers. The content exists to answer questions and provide knowledge. Every optimization technique supports this purpose.

**Characteristics**: Factual, data-driven, clearly structured, answers specific questions, cites sources, provides actionable information.

**Examples**: How-to guides, tutorials, educational articles, research reports, glossaries, knowledge base articles.

### Purpose: Persuade and Convert

**Optimization approach**: Full SEO + Moderate AEO + Light GEO

SEO drives traffic to conversion pages. AEO captures high-intent answer queries ("how much does X cost", "is X worth it"). But conversion copy must prioritize persuasion over answer formatting. Do not flatten compelling sales copy into FAQ format.

**Characteristics**: Benefit-focused, emotional triggers, social proof, calls to action, urgency, value propositions.

**Examples**: Product pages, service pages, landing pages, pricing pages.

**Warning**: Over-optimizing conversion content for AEO/GEO can strip away the emotional elements that actually drive conversions. A pricing page that reads like a Wikipedia entry will not convert.

### Purpose: Build Trust and Authority

**Optimization approach**: Moderate SEO + Light AEO + Full GEO

GEO is the priority here. Entity clarity, brand signals, and cross-platform consistency determine how AI systems perceive your brand. SEO ensures basic discoverability. AEO is mostly irrelevant because trust content rarely answers direct questions.

**Characteristics**: Brand narrative, company history, team credentials, values, partnerships, certifications.

**Examples**: About page, team page, brand story, awards page, press page.

### Purpose: Entertain and Engage

**Optimization approach**: Light SEO + None AEO + Light GEO

Creative content should be creative. Do not force answer formatting on a brand manifesto. Do not add FAQ schema to a storytelling piece. Light SEO ensures basic discoverability. Light GEO only if the content contains genuinely quotable insights or data.

**Characteristics**: Narrative voice, emotional tone, creative freedom, personality, humor, storytelling.

**Examples**: Brand stories, opinion pieces, culture content, behind-the-scenes, personal narratives.

**Warning**: This is where over-optimization does the most damage. A witty, engaging brand voice flattened into "optimized" paragraphs loses its entire purpose. Protect the creative intent.

### Purpose: Guide and Support

**Optimization approach**: Full SEO + Full AEO + Light GEO

Help content is a natural AEO target. Users search for specific problems and want direct answers. Full SEO and AEO. GEO light because help content is usually product-specific and less likely to be cited in general AI answers.

**Characteristics**: Problem-solution structure, step-by-step instructions, troubleshooting, clear and concise.

**Examples**: Help center articles, troubleshooting guides, setup instructions, FAQs.

### Purpose: Announce and Update

**Optimization approach**: Moderate SEO + Light AEO + Light GEO

News and announcements have a short relevance window. Moderate SEO for discoverability during that window. Light AEO/GEO because the content becomes outdated quickly.

**Characteristics**: Timely, factual, brief, newsworthy.

**Examples**: Product updates, company news, press releases, event announcements.

### Purpose: Legal and Compliance

**Optimization approach**: None SEO + None AEO + None GEO

Legal content exists for compliance, not discovery. Do not optimize. Do not add schema. Users find these pages through site navigation, not search.

**Examples**: Privacy policy, terms of service, cookie policy, legal disclaimers.

### Purpose: Internal Operations

**Optimization approach**: None SEO + None AEO + None GEO

Internal pages are not search targets. noindex where appropriate. Focus entirely on the functional purpose.

**Examples**: Login, dashboard, account settings, internal tools, search results pages.

## Decision Flowchart

When unsure which optimization to apply, walk through these questions:

**Step 1: Is this content meant for external discovery?**
- No → None/None/None (legal, internal, login pages)
- Yes → Continue

**Step 2: Does this content answer a specific question?**
- Yes → AEO is relevant (at least Moderate)
- No → AEO is Light or None

**Step 3: Does this content contain authoritative, factual information with sources?**
- Yes → GEO is relevant (at least Moderate)
- No → GEO is Light or None

**Step 4: Is the content's primary purpose creative, emotional, or persuasive?**
- Yes → Reduce AEO/GEO intensity. Protect the creative intent. SEO basics only.
- No → Continue with standard optimization

**Step 5: Is this content evergreen or time-sensitive?**
- Evergreen → Higher optimization investment justified
- Time-sensitive → Moderate optimization, not worth heavy investment

**Step 6: Does this content target a specific search query with measurable volume?**
- Yes → Full SEO
- No → Moderate or Light SEO

## Common Mistakes

### Over-Optimizing

**Turning every page into an FAQ**: Not every page should have question headings and 50-word answer blocks. A brand story with "What is our mission?" as an H2 followed by a 45-word paragraph feels robotic.

**Stripping personality from copy**: Landing page copy that says "Our product helps teams collaborate" instead of a compelling value proposition because someone wanted it "optimized" has lost its conversion power.

**Adding schema to everything**: FAQ schema on a privacy policy page, HowTo schema on an about page. Schema should match actual content purpose, not be added for the sake of having it.

**Forcing self-contained paragraphs everywhere**: GEO extractability principles are for informational content. A product page's emotional hook ("Imagine never losing a file again") is supposed to create desire, not be a self-contained AI-extractable unit.

### Under-Optimizing

**Ignoring SEO on high-traffic-potential pages**: A comprehensive guide with no keyword research, no heading structure, and no meta optimization is invisible to search.

**Skipping AEO on obvious answer content**: An FAQ page without FAQ schema, a how-to guide without structured steps. Easy wins left on the table.

**Neglecting GEO on authoritative content**: Original research with specific data, published without self-contained paragraphs or entity signals, is invisible to AI systems.

### Mismatching

**Full AEO on opinion content**: Your CEO's thought leadership piece should not be formatted as a snippet-bait FAQ. It is an expert perspective, not a factual answer.

**Full GEO on transactional pages**: A checkout page does not need self-contained paragraphs for AI extraction. Focus on conversion UX.

**Full SEO on temporary content**: A one-week promotion page does not need a comprehensive keyword strategy. Basic meta tags and go.

## Quick Reference: When in Doubt

| If the content... | Then prioritize... |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Answers a factual question | AEO (snippet format + schema) |
| Contains original data or research | GEO (self-contained, cited paragraphs) |
| Targets a high-volume search query | SEO (full keyword optimization) |
| Exists to convert visitors | SEO basics + persuasive copy (protect the sales message) |
| Tells a brand story | Creative intent (light SEO at most) |
| Is a legal requirement | Nothing (no optimization needed) |
| Compares products or services | All three (SEO + AEO + GEO) |
| Is a comprehensive guide on a core topic | All three (SEO + AEO + GEO) at full intensity |
| Is time-sensitive news | Moderate SEO + Light AEO (short lifespan) |
| Supports existing customers | SEO + AEO (help them find answers) |
