# SEO Fundamentals Reference

Detailed reference for traditional SEO practices. This covers keyword research, on-page optimization, content strategy, link building, and E-E-A-T.

---

## 1. Keyword Research and Search Intent

### Search Intent Types

Every search query has an underlying intent. Matching content to intent is the single most important factor in SEO content strategy.

| Intent Type | What the User Wants | Content Format | Example Query |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------|---------------|
| Informational | Learn something | Blog post, guide, tutorial, FAQ | "how does solar energy work" |
| Navigational | Find a specific site/page | Homepage, product page, login page | "spotify login" |
| Commercial Investigation | Compare before buying | Comparison, review, listicle | "best crm for small business" |
| Transactional | Complete an action (buy, sign up) | Product page, pricing page, checkout | "buy macbook air m3" |

### Keyword Research Process

1. **Seed keywords**: Start with core topics your business covers. Think about what problems you solve, what products/services you offer, and what questions your customers ask.

2. **Expand with tools**: Use keyword research tools (Google Keyword Planner, Semrush, Ahrefs, etc.) to find related terms, questions, and long-tail variations. Also mine:
   - Google autocomplete suggestions
   - "People also ask" boxes
   - Related searches at bottom of SERPs
   - Competitor keyword analysis
   - Reddit/Quora/forum questions in your space

3. **Evaluate keywords on three dimensions**:
   - **Search volume**: How many people search this monthly
   - **Keyword difficulty**: How hard it is to rank (based on competitor strength)
   - **Business relevance**: How closely it maps to your product/service/audience

4. **Map intent to content**: Group keywords by intent and assign appropriate content formats. Do not try to rank a blog post for a transactional query, or a product page for an informational query.

5. **Prioritize**: Focus on keywords where you have a realistic chance of ranking (reasonable difficulty), meaningful search volume, and strong business relevance. Do not chase high-volume vanity keywords you cannot rank for.

### Keyword Mapping

Every target page should have:
- **Primary keyword**: The main term you are optimizing for (one per page)
- **Secondary keywords**: 2-5 closely related terms that support the primary
- **Long-tail variations**: Specific queries and questions the page can answer
- **Semantic relatives**: Topically related terms that demonstrate comprehensive coverage

Avoid keyword cannibalization: do not target the same primary keyword with multiple pages. If it is happening, consolidate or differentiate the pages.

## 2. On-Page Optimization

### Title Tags
- Include primary keyword, ideally near the beginning
- Keep under 60 characters to avoid truncation in SERPs
- Make it compelling, this is your headline in search results
- Each page needs a unique title
- Include brand name at the end if space allows (e.g., "Primary Keyword Topic | Brand")

### Meta Descriptions
- Summarize the page value proposition in 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally (Google bolds matching terms)
- Include a call to action or value hook
- Each page needs a unique meta description
- Google may override your meta description with content from the page, that is normal

### Heading Structure
- **H1**: One per page, contains primary keyword, describes the main topic
- **H2**: Major sections of the page, include secondary keywords where natural
- **H3-H6**: Subsections for organization and readability
- Headings should create a logical outline of the content
- Do not skip heading levels for cosmetic reasons (H1 to H3 without H2)

### Content Quality Signals
- **Comprehensive coverage**: Address the topic thoroughly enough that users do not need to go elsewhere
- **Original insight**: Add value beyond what is already ranking, such as proprietary data, unique experience, expert perspective
- **Freshness**: Update content when information changes. Add "last updated" dates where relevant
- **Readability**: Short paragraphs, clear language, logical flow. Break up walls of text.
- **Multimedia**: Images, videos, charts, tables where they add value (not decoration)

### Internal Linking
- Link from high-authority pages to pages you want to boost
- Use descriptive anchor text that indicates what the linked page is about
- Create logical pathways: related content should link to each other
- Ensure every important page is reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage
- Fix orphan pages (pages with no internal links pointing to them)
- Avoid excessive links on a single page, prioritize the most relevant

### URL Structure
- Keep URLs short, descriptive, and readable
- Use hyphens to separate words (not underscores)
- Include primary keyword where natural
- Use logical directory structure that reflects site hierarchy
- Avoid unnecessary parameters, session IDs, or random strings
- Use lowercase consistently

## 3. Content Strategy

### Topic Clusters (Hub and Spoke)

Organize content around topic clusters to build topical authority:

- **Pillar page (hub)**: Comprehensive overview of a broad topic. Covers the topic at a high level and links out to cluster content.
- **Cluster pages (spokes)**: In-depth content on specific subtopics. Each links back to the pillar and to related cluster pages.

Example for a CRM company:
- Pillar: "The Complete Guide to CRM"
- Clusters: "CRM for Small Business", "How to Choose a CRM", "CRM Implementation Best Practices", "CRM vs Spreadsheets", "CRM Integration Guide"

This structure signals to search engines that you have comprehensive expertise on the topic.

### Content Gap Analysis

1. Identify keywords your competitors rank for that you do not
2. Identify questions your audience asks that you have not answered
3. Identify content formats your competitors use that you do not (video, tools, templates)
4. Prioritize gaps by business impact and feasibility

### Content Types by Funnel Stage

| Funnel Stage | User Need | Content Types |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| Awareness | Understand a problem or topic | Blog posts, educational guides, infographics, videos |
| Consideration | Evaluate solutions | Comparison guides, case studies, webinars, whitepapers |
| Decision | Choose a specific solution | Product pages, pricing, demos, reviews, testimonials |
| Retention | Get value from purchase | Help docs, tutorials, community content, newsletters |

### Content Freshness

- Audit existing content quarterly for accuracy and relevance
- Update statistics, links, and examples when they become outdated
- Consolidate thin or overlapping content into stronger pieces
- Remove or redirect content that no longer serves a purpose
- Add "last updated" dates to evergreen content

## 4. Link Building

### Why Backlinks Matter

Backlinks remain one of Google's strongest ranking signals. A link from another site to yours acts as a vote of confidence. Quality matters far more than quantity. One link from an authoritative, relevant site outweighs hundreds of low-quality links.

### Link Quality Assessment

Evaluate potential backlinks on:
- **Relevance**: Is the linking site/page topically related to yours?
- **Authority**: Does the linking site have its own strong backlink profile and reputation?
- **Traffic**: Does the linking site receive real organic traffic?
- **Placement**: Editorial links within content are more valuable than footer/sidebar links
- **Anchor text**: Natural, varied anchor text is healthiest. Avoid over-optimized exact-match anchors.

### Link Building Strategies

**Content-driven (most sustainable)**:
- Create linkable assets: original research, data studies, tools, comprehensive guides, infographics
- Publish industry surveys and share results
- Create free tools or calculators relevant to your audience
- Develop thought leadership content that others want to reference

**Outreach-based**:
- Digital PR: Create newsworthy content and pitch to journalists
- Guest posting: Contribute expert content to relevant publications
- Broken link building: Find broken links on relevant sites and suggest your content as a replacement
- Resource page link building: Get listed on curated resource pages
- Unlinked brand mentions: Find mentions of your brand without links and request them

**Relationship-based**:
- Build genuine relationships in your industry
- Participate in communities, podcasts, events, and collaborations
- Co-create content with partners, customers, and industry peers
- Provide testimonials to tools/services you use (often results in a link)

### What to Avoid
- Buying links or participating in link schemes
- PBNs (private blog networks)
- Mass directory submissions
- Low-quality guest posting purely for links
- Excessive link exchanges
- Auto-generated or spun content for links

These violate Google's spam policies and risk manual penalties.

## 5. E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

### What E-E-A-T Is

E-E-A-T is not a direct ranking factor or algorithm. It is a framework from Google's Search Quality Rater Guidelines that describes the qualities Google's systems are designed to reward. Content that demonstrates these qualities tends to perform better in search.

### How to Demonstrate E-E-A-T

**Experience**:
- Show first-hand experience with the topic (personal anecdotes, real usage, photos of actual experience)
- Include case studies with real results
- Share lessons learned from doing, not just researching

**Expertise**:
- Author bios with relevant credentials and background
- Content depth that reflects genuine knowledge
- Accurate, well-researched information with citations
- Clear explanations that demonstrate understanding

**Authoritativeness**:
- Earn mentions and links from other respected sources in your field
- Build a consistent publishing track record on your core topics
- Author profiles linked to relevant professional presence (LinkedIn, publications, speaking engagements)
- Brand mentions across industry platforms

**Trustworthiness**:
- Transparent about who you are (clear About page, contact info, physical address if applicable)
- Accurate, factual content with sources cited
- Secure site (HTTPS)
- Clear editorial policies and content standards
- Honest product/service information (do not oversell)
- Privacy policy, terms of service present and accessible

### YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)

E-E-A-T scrutiny is higher for topics that can impact users' health, financial stability, safety, or well-being. Examples: medical advice, financial planning, legal information, news about current events. For YMYL topics, credentialing and sourcing must be especially strong.
