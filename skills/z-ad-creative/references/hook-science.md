# Hook Science: Categories, Formulas, and Benchmarks

The hook is the single most important element of any ad. Ogilvy said writing
the headline spends "80 cents of your dollar." Modern data confirms this:
ads with optimized hooks see 30-50% lower CPA because they feed more qualified
traffic into the funnel.

This reference covers: hook metrics, 12 hook categories with templates, and
platform-specific hook strategy.

## Hook Metrics & Benchmarks {#metrics}

### Definitions

| Metric | Platform | Formula |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Hook Rate** | TikTok | 2-second video views / impressions |
| **Hook Rate** | Meta | 3-second video views / impressions |
| **Hold Rate** | TikTok | 6-second focused-view rate / 2-second views |
| **Hold Rate** | Meta | ThruPlays / 3-second views |

### Benchmarks (2025-2026)

| Metric | Platform | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|--------|----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| Hook Rate | TikTok | <20% | 25-30% | 30-40% | 40%+ |
| Hook Rate | Meta | <15% | 20-25% | 25-30% | 30%+ |
| Hold Rate | Both | <15% | 20-25% | 25-35% | 35%+ |

### Why Hook Rate Matters

- 90% of TikTok ad recall impact occurs in the first 6 seconds
- Memory embedding requires 2.5 seconds of attention (research from The Research Agency)
- 60% higher total retention leads to delivery boosts and cheaper impressions
- Strong hook + weak hold = fix the body; Weak hook = nothing else matters

### Diagnostic Table

| Hook Rate | Hold Rate | Diagnosis | Fix |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Low | Low | Wrong audience or weak creative | Rethink angle + hook |
| Low | High | Good content, bad packaging | Rewrite hook only |
| High | Low | Clickbait — promising but not delivering | Add pattern interrupts, tighten body |
| High | High | Winner — scale it | Create variations of this format |

## 12 Hook Categories {#categories}

### 1. Problem Call-Out
Name the audience's pain directly. Works for Problem-Aware audiences.

**Pattern**: "[Specific pain] is costing you [specific consequence]"

**Examples**:
- "You're losing 4 hours a week to manual data entry"
- "Still copying and pasting between 6 different tools?"
- "Your support team answers the same 50 questions every day"

### 2. Surprising Statistic
Lead with a number that challenges assumptions. Creates a cognitive pause.

**Pattern**: "[Unexpected number] + [what it means for them]"

**Examples**:
- "70% of startups fail because of premature scaling — not bad products"
- "The average person sees 10,000 ads per day. Only 3 get clicked."
- "Teams waste 31 hours per month in unnecessary meetings"

### 3. Curiosity Gap
Open a loop that demands closure. The brain cannot leave a gap unfilled.

**Pattern**: "The [unexpected method] that [desirable result]" or "Why [common thing] is [counterintuitive claim]"

**Examples**:
- "The one Gmail setting that cut my inbox time by 80%"
- "Why the best salespeople never talk about the product"
- "3 things your accountant won't tell you about tax deductions"

### 4. Contrarian/Hot Take
Challenge a widely-held belief. Forces the viewer to engage (even if just
to disagree). Best in saturated markets where differentiation is hard.

**Pattern**: "Stop [common advice]. Here's what actually works."

**Examples**:
- "Forget A/B testing. Here's what top marketers do instead."
- "Posting daily on LinkedIn is killing your reach. Here's why."
- "Your morning routine is not the problem"

### 5. Before/After Transformation
Show the contrast between pain and outcome. Visual for video/carousel,
verbal for text.

**Pattern**: "[Before state] → [After state]"

**Examples**:
- "From 200 unread emails to inbox zero by 9 AM"
- "Before: 3 hours building reports. After: 3 minutes."
- Video: split-screen showing messy desk vs. organized workspace

### 6. Social Proof Lead
Lead with the crowd. Numbers and names create instant credibility.

**Pattern**: "[Number] of [identity group] already [desirable action]"

**Examples**:
- "12,847 founders switched to [product] this quarter"
- "Rated #1 on G2 for the 4th year running"
- "Here's why Y Combinator companies use [product]"

### 7. Direct Address / Identity
Call out the specific person. People stop scrolling when they feel seen.

**Pattern**: "If you're a [identity] who [specific situation]..."

**Examples**:
- "Attention: freelancers who hate invoicing"
- "This is for the founder running everything from a spreadsheet"
- "Solo developers: stop building auth from scratch"

### 8. Story/Narrative
Open with a personal story. The brain processes narrative 22x more memorably
than facts alone (Stanford research).

**Pattern**: "I [personal situation] until [discovery]..."

**Examples**:
- "I was about to shut down my startup when I tried one thing..."
- "6 months ago I couldn't get a single reply to my cold emails"
- "My co-founder quit. I had 30 days of runway. Then this happened."

### 9. Question Hook
Ask a question they must answer mentally. Forces engagement.

**Pattern**: "Are you still [outdated approach]?" or "What if [desirable outcome] was possible?"

**Examples**:
- "What would you do with 10 extra hours every week?"
- "Are you still tracking leads in a spreadsheet?"
- "Want to know the #1 reason your ads aren't converting?"

### 10. Demonstration/Show Don't Tell
For video: show the product doing something impressive in 3 seconds.
The visual IS the hook.

**Pattern**: [Visual demonstration] + minimal text

**Examples**:
- Screen recording: type a prompt, watch the AI generate a full email
- Before/after product demo with timer showing speed
- Side-by-side: manual process vs. automated process

### 11. Urgency/Scarcity
Time pressure forces immediate action. Use only when genuine.

**Pattern**: "[Specific deadline] + [what they'll miss]"

**Examples**:
- "This pricing disappears at midnight"
- "Only 47 early-bird spots left"
- "Last day: 50% off annual plans"

### 12. Pattern Interrupt
Something visually or tonally unexpected that breaks the scroll pattern.
Works for Unaware audiences who need to be jolted out of autopilot.

**Pattern**: [Unexpected visual/format] + [bridge to message]

**Examples**:
- Starting a video mid-sentence, as if the viewer walked into a conversation
- Upside-down text or inverted colors
- "Don't buy this product." (reverse psychology)
- Handwritten note style instead of polished design

## Platform-Specific Hook Strategy {#platform-hooks}

### TikTok
- Hook must land in first 1-2 seconds (before skip)
- Native feel: no branded intro, no logo animation
- Top performers: direct address, story, demonstration
- Use TikTok-native text overlays, not designed graphics
- Pattern interrupt works exceptionally well on FYP

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- Primary text: first 125 characters before "See more" are the hook
- Image ads: the image itself is the hook — make it thumb-stopping
- Video: first 3 seconds determine everything
- Carousel: first card is the hook — if it fails, no one swipes

### Google Search
- Headline 1 is the hook — must include the search keyword
- Headline 2 is the value prop
- Headline 3 is proof or CTA
- Specificity wins: "Save 4.2 Hours/Week" beats "Save Time"

### LinkedIn
- First 150 characters of intro text are visible (mobile)
- Professional insight hooks outperform promotional hooks
- Data and research hooks perform best for B2B
- Thought Leader Ads: hook should sound like a personal revelation, not an ad

### Display
- The entire ad is the hook — it must communicate in <2 seconds
- Bold visual + 5-7 word headline + CTA
- Contrast with surrounding content (but respect platform guidelines)

## Video Hook Structure {#video-hooks}

### The 3-Second Rule

| Second | Goal | What Happens |
|--------|------|-------------|
| 0-1 | Stop the scroll | Visual pattern interrupt, face, movement, text |
| 1-2 | Create curiosity | Verbal hook: question, claim, or story opener |
| 2-3 | Promise value | Why should they keep watching? |

### Video Hook Enhancers
- **Eye contact**: Person looking directly at camera in first frame
- **Movement**: Physical motion in first frame (walking, turning, gesturing)
- **Text overlay**: Reinforces the verbal hook for sound-off viewers
- **Quick cuts**: 2-3 second cuts maintain attention after the hook
- **Sound design**: Unexpected sound effect in first second (for sound-on viewers)

## Static/Text Hook Patterns {#text-hooks}

### Headline Writing Rules (Ogilvy + Modern Data)

1. **Write 15+ before picking**: Ogilvy wrote "at least 16 headlines" for every ad
2. **Include a benefit**: Unless your headline sells, you've wasted 90% of your money
3. **Use specific numbers**: "127% increase" beats "huge increase"
4. **Include news when possible**: Headlines with news are "surefire" (Ogilvy)
5. **Avoid cleverness**: Clear beats clever every time. No puns in headlines.
6. **Quote marks boost recall**: Headlines in quotation marks get 28% more recall
7. **Front-load the important words**: Mobile users scan the first 4-5 words

### Power Words for Hooks
**Curiosity**: secret, hidden, unknown, surprising, unexpected, strange
**Urgency**: now, today, limited, final, last chance, before
**Specificity**: exactly, step-by-step, the 3 ways, in 7 minutes
**Social proof**: everyone, thousands, trending, rated #1, award-winning
**Benefit**: free, save, guaranteed, proven, instant, easy

## Hook Testing Protocol {#testing}

### Process
1. Write 5+ hooks for every brief (different categories from the 12 above)
2. Run each as a separate ad with identical body + CTA + creative
3. Measure hook rate at 48 hours / $100-200 spend per variant
4. Kill hooks below platform benchmark
5. Create 3 new variations of the winning hook category
6. Repeat until you find a 30%+ hook rate performer

### Common Hook Failures
| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Low hook rate across all variants | Wrong audience or wrong angle | Test different awareness levels |
| One hook works, rest fail | Found the right emotional trigger | Double down on that trigger category |
| High hook rate, low conversion | Hook promises something body doesn't deliver | Align body to hook's promise |
